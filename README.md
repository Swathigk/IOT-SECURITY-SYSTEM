##In this project, I created and developed an IoT based smart security prototype with the use of an Arduino microcontroller inside the Tinkercad simulation platform. It was aimed at developing a system which would be able to sense movement within a given area and raise an alarm whenever movement is detected. This piece of work represents the basic principles of modern smart security devices that are used in homes, businesses, and high-security environments.It is designed to be based on a Passive Infrared (PIR) motion sensor which detects variations in infrared radiation emitted by living objects. As an individual passes through the field of view of the sensor, the PIR responds to the temperature difference, emits a pulse, and sends it to the Arduino board. The pulse is then interpreted by the control unit and an alert sequence is started.The simulated circuit PIR is connected to an Arduino Uno: The signal line of the PIR is connected to digital pin 7, the supply line of the PIR is connected to the 5 V rail, and the ground line of the PIR is connected to the Arduino ground. The status of motion is reflected by a red LED with a current limiting resistor on digital pin 13 and a small buzzer will give an audible alarm on digital pin 8. These two components are brought back to the ground, and the loop is completed.The drawing constantly checks the output of the PIR. When the motion is detected it powers the LED and diverts the buzzer and thus indicates that an activity has taken place within the perimeter being monitored. It also sends a message to the serial console correspondingly, which would show how a real IoT system would communicate alerts or notifications to an external device or central monitoring hub.The process allowed me to internalise the principles of operation of motion-detection hardware and the complexities of microcontroller-based automation without having to have actual hardware.Altogether, this project extended my practical knowledge in the area of Arduino programming, sensor combination, and the schematic layout. It also strengthened my understanding of fundamental IoT security principles. The prototype demonstrates the power of small electronic components to be combined to create an effective motion detection and alert system- a starting point that can be expanded to create a full smart security system.
